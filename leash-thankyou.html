10-Day Training Plan — Leash Walking Mastery

A gentle, structured programme to teach your dog to walk calmly by your side without pulling. Short walks · Clear exercises · Real-life practice

This is a digital training plan included in your purchase. You can follow each day directly on this page.

Day 1 — Equipment & Safe Start (Set up)
Choose safe, comfortable equipment and introduce it positively.

  • Fit a well-fitting harness or flat collar (no choke/prong tools).
  • Let your dog wear the gear indoors with treats and praise.
  • Keep the first session very short and positive.

Day 2 — Reward “By My Side” (Foundation)
Teach your dog that staying near your leg brings rewards.

  • Stand still, mark and reward when your dog appears near your left or right leg.
  • Add a cue like “Close” once your dog understands.
  • Keep 3–4 mini sessions, 2–3 minutes each.

Day 3 — First Loose-Leash Steps Indoors (Low distraction)
Start practising calm, short-distance walking where distractions are minimal.

  • Take a few slow steps indoors and reward when the leash stays loose.
  • If your dog surges forward, simply stop and reset without tension.
  • Finish each tiny session with praise and play.

Day 4 — Introduce Direction Changes (Follow the handler)
Teach your dog to follow your movements calmly.

  • Walk forward 3–4 steps, then turn left or right gently.
  • Reward your dog for staying with you through the turn.
  • Avoid pulling — your movement should guide the behaviour.

Day 5 — Controlled Outdoor Start (New environment)
Move outdoors but choose a quiet area with fewer distractions.

  • Take only 1–2 minutes of walking before giving a break.
  • Reward check-ins (when your dog looks at you willingly).
  • Keep the leash loose; avoid correcting or yanking.

Day 6 — Build Focus Around Mild Distractions (Progression)
Introduce gentle distractions while keeping expectations low.

  • Walk near mild triggers such as distant people or parked bicycles.
  • Use “Look” or “Close” when your dog becomes slightly aroused.
  • Reward calm behaviour before your dog escalates.

Day 7 — Pattern Walking (Structure)
Use predictable walking patterns to strengthen your dog’s confidence.

  • Try figure-8s, loops or straight-line patterns in a quiet area.
  • Reward your dog for staying beside you through the pattern.
  • Repeat 2–3 short sessions.

Day 8 — Introduce Real-World Challenges (Applied practice)
Start walking in slightly busier environments.

  • Walk past driveways, moving cars at a distance, or mild street noise.
  • Reward frequently when your dog chooses loose-leash walking.
  • Move away if your dog becomes overstimulated.

Day 9 — Extend Duration & Confidence (Stamina)
Increase the total walking time while maintaining loose leash behaviour.

  • Begin with short 1–2 minute intervals, adding time slowly.
  • Use calm praise and occasional treats to reinforce good choices.
  • End on success — don't wait until your dog is tired or frustrated.

Day 10 — Real-World Walk & Long-Term Plan (Maintain)
Put everything together in a normal walk and build a long-term routine.

  • Choose a calm route and practise all skills from previous days.
  • Reward often for loose leash and voluntary check-ins.
  • Plan 2–3 “training walks” per week to maintain progress.
