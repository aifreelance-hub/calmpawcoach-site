7-Day Training Plan — Stop Excessive Barking

Short daily sessions · Positive training · Clear progress every day

Follow this simple, step-by-step plan to reduce your dog's excessive barking in just 7 days. This is a digital training plan included in your CalmPaw Complete Pack. You can read it directly on this page from any device.

Day 1 — Track & Understand the Barking (Observation day)
Start by understanding when and why your dog barks.

  • Write down the time, trigger, and duration of each barking episode.
  • Notice patterns: visitors, outside noises, boredom, separation, etc.
  • Keep sessions calm — today is about observing, not correcting.

Day 2 — Teach a Calm “Focus on Me” Cue (Foundation skill)

  • We teach your dog to look at you on cue so you can interrupt barking before it escalates.
  • Say your marker word (e.g. “Yes”) and reward when your dog looks at you.
  • Add a cue like “Look” after a few repetitions.
  • Practice 3–5 short sessions of 3 minutes each.

Day 3 — Introduce Controlled Triggers (Low-level practice)

  • Gently introduce mild versions of barking triggers while reinforcing calm behavior.
  • Play quiet doorbell sounds on your phone or create small outside noises.
  • Before your dog reacts, use “Look” and reward eye contact.
  • End before your dog gets overwhelmed.

Day 4 — Reward Quiet Moments (Change the habit)

  • Today you reward silence on purpose, so your dog learns that quiet pays.
  • Keep treats ready when you expect barking triggers.
  • When your dog chooses not to bark or stops quickly, quietly praise and reward.
  • Ignore attention-seeking barking as much as possible.

Day 5 — Add a “Quiet” Cue (Name the behavior)

  • Once your dog understands that calm is good, you add a clear “Quiet” cue.
  • Wait for a break in barking, calmly say “Quiet”, then reward.
  • Practice in very short bursts; never shout the cue.
  • Repeat with different low-level triggers.

Day 6 — Practice in Real-Life Situations (Generalisation)

  • Start using your new skills in normal daily situations.
  • Ask a friend to ring the bell or walk past your door.
  • Use “Look” and “Quiet” before barking escalates.
  • Reward generously for calm choices.

Day 7 — Review & Long-Term Plan (Maintain progress)

  • Today you review progress and create a simple routine to keep barking under control.
  • Decide on 1–2 daily “calm training” moments you will keep.
  • Note what helped your dog the most this week.
  • Continue to reward quiet behaviour — good habits grow with practice.

10-Day Training Plan — Leash Walking Mastery

Short walks · Clear exercises · Real-life practice

A gentle, structured programme to teach your dog to walk calmly by your side without pulling. This is a digital training plan included in your CalmPaw Complete Pack. You can follow each day directly on this page.

Day 1 — Equipment & Safe Start (Set up)

  • Choose safe, comfortable equipment and introduce it positively.
  • Fit a well-fitting harness or flat collar (no choke/prong tools).
  • Let your dog wear the gear indoors with treats and praise.
  • Keep the first session very short and positive.

Day 2 — Reward “By My Side” (Foundation)

  • Teach your dog that staying near your leg brings rewards.
  • Stand still, mark and reward when your dog appears near your left or right leg.
  • Add a cue like “Close” once your dog understands.
  • Keep 3–4 mini sessions, 2–3 minutes each.

Day 3 — First Loose-Leash Steps Indoors (Low distraction)

  • Start practising calm, short-distance walking where distractions are minimal.
  • Take a few slow steps indoors and reward when the leash stays loose.
  • If your dog surges forward, simply stop and reset without tension.
  • Finish each tiny session with praise and play.

Day 4 — Introduce Direction Changes (Follow the handler)

  • Teach your dog to follow your movements calmly.
  • Walk forward 3–4 steps, then turn left or right gently.
  • Reward your dog for staying with you through the turn.
  • Avoid pulling — your movement should guide the behaviour.

Day 5 — Controlled Outdoor Start (New environment)

  • Move outdoors but choose a quiet area with fewer distractions.
  • Take only 1–2 minutes of walking before giving a break.
  • Reward check-ins (when your dog looks at you willingly).
  • Keep the leash loose; avoid correcting or yanking.

Day 6 — Build Focus Around Mild Distractions (Progression)

  • Introduce gentle distractions while keeping expectations low.
  • Walk near mild triggers such as distant people or parked bicycles.
  • Use “Look” or “Close” when your dog becomes slightly aroused.
  • Reward calm behaviour before your dog escalates.

Day 7 — Pattern Walking (Structure)

  • Use predictable walking patterns to strengthen your dog’s confidence.
  • Try figure-8s, loops or straight-line patterns in a quiet area.
  • Reward your dog for staying beside you through the pattern.
  • Repeat 2–3 short sessions.

Day 8 — Introduce Real-World Challenges (Applied practice)

  • Start walking in slightly busier environments.
  • Walk past driveways, moving cars at a distance, or mild street noise.
  • Reward frequently when your dog chooses loose-leash walking.
  • Move away if your dog becomes overstimulated.

Day 9 — Extend Duration & Confidence (Stamina)

  • Increase the total walking time while maintaining loose leash behaviour.
  • Begin with short 1–2 minute intervals, adding time slowly.
  • Use calm praise and occasional treats to reinforce good choices.
  • End on success — don't wait until your dog is tired or frustrated.

Day 10 — Real-World Walk & Long-Term Plan (Maintain)

  • Put everything together in a normal walk and build a long-term routine.
  • Choose a calm route and practise all skills from previous days.
  • Reward often for loose leash and voluntary check-ins.
  • Plan 2–3 “training walks” per week to maintain progress.

14-Day Training Plan — Separation Anxiety Reset

Tiny absences · Calm routines · Gradual confidence building

A gentle step-by-step schedule to help your dog feel safer and calmer when home alone. This is a digital training plan included in your CalmPaw Complete Pack. Follow one small step each day.

Day 1 — Safety Check & Baseline (Foundation)

  • Choose a quiet, safe area where your dog will stay when alone.
  • Provide water, a comfy bed, and a safe chew.
  • Record how long your dog can stay calm when you step out of sight.

Day 2 — Micro Absences (Seconds only)

  • Step out of the room for 5–10 seconds, then return calmly.
  • Repeat several times, always returning while your dog is still relatively calm.
  • Do 2–3 short sessions per day.

Day 3 — Predictable Leaving Routine (Structure)

  • Teach your dog that your leaving routine is calm and predictable.
  • Pick a simple routine (shoes → key → calm word → leave).
  • Practise 10–15 repetitions without actually leaving the home.
  • Reward calm behaviour during this routine.

Day 4 — Calm Alone Moments (First distance)

  • Start building tiny “alone moments” inside your home.
  • Walk to the other side of the room for 10–20 seconds.
  • Return calmly before your dog becomes anxious.
  • Repeat 5–6 times.

Day 5 — Add Mild Sounds (Low-level challenge)

  • Introduce gentle background noises while your dog is alone briefly.
  • Soft door sounds, quiet footsteps, or distant outside noise.
  • Keep absences short and end before anxiety builds.
  • Reward calmness with soft praise on return.

Day 6 — Increase Absence Duration (Progression)

  • Extend absence from 10–20 seconds to 30–45 seconds.
  • Move slowly — only increase duration if your dog stays calm.
  • Return immediately if anxiety rises.
  • Do 3–4 short sessions today.

Day 7 — Build Consistency (Routine)

  • Reinforce your dog’s confidence with predictable daily practice.
  • Practise 5–6 micro absences throughout the day.
  • Keep your leaving routine identical every time.
  • Avoid emotional greetings.

Day 8 — First 1–2 Minute Absence (Milestone)

  • Increase duration into the first meaningful absence territory.
  • Try 60–90 seconds away from your dog.
  • Monitor signs of stress — pacing, whining, licking.
  • Stop immediately if your dog becomes distressed.

Day 9 — Calm Independence Skills (Confidence)

  • Teach your dog to relax independently, even when you’re home.
  • Use scatter feeding or a chew toy during short solo moments.
  • Reward your dog for choosing to rest calmly.
  • Ignore demand barking for attention.

Day 10 — Moderate Absences (Stability)

  • Build duration gradually now that your dog has a foundation.
  • Practise 2–3 minute absences in several repetitions.
  • Keep everything predictable and calm.
  • Reward quiet behaviour softly after each return.

Day 11 — Door Practice (Realistic setup)

  • Help your dog understand that door sounds do not mean panic.
  • Open and close the door lightly during absence practice.
  • Use your calm cue if your dog becomes alert.
  • Keep absences under your dog’s panic threshold.

Day 12 — Short Outdoor Absences (Next level)

  • Practise leaving the house briefly for the first time.
  • Step outside for 30–60 seconds, then return quietly.
  • Do not ring the doorbell — keep triggers minimal.
  • Repeat 3–4 times.

Day 13 — Build Real-World Duration (Strengthening)

  • Increase outside absences to 2–4 minutes if your dog is coping well.
  • Keep your leaving routine the same every time.
  • Only extend duration when your dog remains calm.
  • Avoid emotional returns.

Day 14 — Review & Long-Term Plan (Maintain)

  • Review your dog’s progress and create a sustainable long-term plan.
  • Write down what triggers still cause worry and avoid pushing too far.
  • Plan 3–4 calm-alone sessions per week going forward.
  • Seek a qualified behaviourist if your dog still shows severe anxiety.
