My dog barks every time someone knocks on the door. What should I do?
First, thank you for your question — this is one of the most common behavior issues in dogs and can be improved quickly with consistent training.

Here's a 3-step exercise you can start today:

1️⃣ Controlled Knock Practice
Ask a friend to knock softly. The moment your dog stays calm, reward with a treat. Repeat 5–7 times.

2️⃣ “Quiet” Cue Introduction
When your dog begins to bark, calmly say “Quiet” and wait for 1–2 seconds of silence → reward immediately.

3️⃣ Increase Knock Intensity
Gradually increase the volume of the knock as your dog improves.

He also gets anxious when I leave home. Can AI help with that?
Absolutely — separation anxiety is highly trainable with structured daily exercises. Here's a simple starting routine:

• Warm-up Desensitization
Pick up your keys, put on shoes → sit down again. Repeat until your dog stops reacting.

• Short Absence Training
Step outside for 10–20 seconds, return calmly, no excitement. Repeat 8–10 times.

• Build to Longer Absences
Slowly increase to 2–3 minutes once your dog stays relaxed.
