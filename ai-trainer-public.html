My dog barks every time someone knocks on the door. What should I do?
Thank you for your question — this is a very common behavior issue and can be improved with consistent training.

Here are three practical training steps you can begin today:

1️⃣ Controlled Knock Exercise
Ask a friend to knock softly. Reward your dog the moment they remain calm. Repeat several times to build familiarity.

2️⃣ Introduce a “Quiet” Cue
When barking begins, say “Quiet” calmly and wait for 1–2 seconds of silence. Reward immediately.

3️⃣ Gradual Intensity Training
Slowly increase the volume of the knock as your dog becomes more confident.
He also gets anxious when I leave home. What should I do first?
Separation anxiety is very common and responds well to structured, gradual exposure training.

• Desensitization Warm-Up
Pick up your keys or bag, then sit back down. Repeat until your dog stops reacting.

• Short Absence Training
Step outside for 10–20 seconds and return calmly. Repeat several times.

• Extend Duration
Once your dog remains relaxed, gradually increase the absence time.
